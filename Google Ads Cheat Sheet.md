## 🧠 1. Core Ad Types (What You Design For)

### 🔎 Search Ads (Text-Based)

* No visuals (BUT affects landing page design)
* Key elements:

  * Headlines (30 chars × 3)
  * Descriptions (90 chars × 2)

👉 Designer role:

* Optimize **landing page UX/UI**
* Match **visual consistency** with ad messaging

---

### 🖼️ Display Ads (Banner Ads)

**Most common sizes:**

* 300×250 (Medium Rectangle) ⭐
* 336×280 (Large Rectangle)
* 728×90 (Leaderboard)
* 300×600 (Half Page) ⭐
* 320×100 (Mobile)

**Rules:**

* Max file size: 150 KB
* Formats: JPG, PNG, GIF (HTML5 for advanced)

---

### 📱 App Ads

* Uses assets (images, videos, text)
* Google auto-generates combinations

👉 Designer:

* Provide **multiple assets** (not one final ad)

---

### 🎥 Video Ads (YouTube)

Formats:

* Skippable (5s+)
* Non-skippable (15–20s)
* Bumper ads (6s)

👉 Key rule:

* **Hook in first 3–5 seconds**

---

### 🧩 Performance Max / Responsive Ads

Assets you provide:

* Images (landscape + square)
* Logos
* Headlines & descriptions
* Videos (optional but recommended)

👉 Google auto-mixes → design must be **modular**

---

## 🎨 2. Design Principles for High-Performing Ads

### 🧲 Visual Hierarchy

* 1 focal point (product / offer)
* Clear reading flow:

  * Headline → Value → CTA

### ⚡ Clarity > Creativity

* Avoid overdesign
* Message understood in **<1 second**

### 🏷️ Branding

* Logo always visible
* Consistent colors & typography

### 📢 CTA (Call-To-Action)

* Strong, action-oriented:

  * “Buy Now”
  * “Get Quote”
  * “Learn More”

---

## 📐 3. Responsive Design Mindset

Design like a **system, not a single banner**:

* Safe zones (center-weighted composition)
* Avoid edge text (cropping issues)
* Use scalable elements

👉 Think:

* “Will this work if cropped to square or mobile?”

---

## 🎯 4. Conversion-Focused Design

### What increases CTR:

* Faces (human element)
* Contrast (background vs CTA)
* Motion (subtle animation / GIF)
* Clear offer (discount, urgency)

### What kills performance:

* Clutter
* Weak contrast
* Too much text
* Generic stock visuals

---

## 🧪 5. A/B Testing for Designers

Always design variants:

* Different:

  * Backgrounds
  * CTA colors
  * Headlines (short vs long)
  * Image vs illustration

👉 Rule:

* Change **ONE variable at a time**

---

## 📏 6. Safe Design Checklist

* Logo visible
* CTA clear and clickable-looking
* Text readable on mobile
* High contrast
* File size optimized
* Works in multiple aspect ratios
* No important elements near edges

---

## 🚫 7. Common Mistakes

* Treating ads like posters (too complex)
* Ignoring mobile
* No visual hierarchy
* Overusing text
* Not aligning with landing page

---

## 🔗 8. Designer → Developer Handoff

Provide:

* Exported assets (all sizes or components)
* Source files (Figma/PSD)
* Font & color specs
* Animation specs (if HTML5/GIF)
* Asset variations (for responsive ads)

---

## ⚡ 9. Pro Tips (Advanced)

* Design with **“thumb-stopping” mindset**
* Use **directional cues** (arrows, gaze)
* Place CTA in **bottom-right or center**
* Test **dark vs light backgrounds**
* Leverage **brand familiarity** (repeat exposure)
