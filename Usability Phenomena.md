## $${\color{red}Usability \ Phenomena}$$ 
#### UX psychology, behavioral patterns, and interaction principles useful for designers, developers, and product teams.


#### Usability phenomena refer to the specific, observable, or perceived behaviors and outcomes that occur during the interaction between a user and a product (such as a website, application, or tool). These can be positive (e.g., fast task completion) or negative (e.g., user frustration, errors)
---

# 🧠 Usability Phenomena — Extended Cheat Sheet

## 👁️ Perception & Attention

### 1. **Hick’s Law**

* Decision time increases with number of choices
  👉 Reduce options, group items, use progressive disclosure

### 2. **Fitts’s Law**

* Time to reach a target depends on size & distance
  👉 Bigger buttons, closer actions = faster interaction

### 3. **Von Restorff Effect (Isolation Effect)**

* Distinct items are remembered more
  👉 Highlight primary CTA

### 4. **Banner Blindness**

* Users ignore anything that looks like ads
  👉 Avoid “ad-like” UI patterns

### 5. **Change Blindness**

* Users don’t notice UI changes without cues
  👉 Use animations, highlights, feedback

---

## 🧠 Cognitive Load & Memory

### 6. **Miller’s Law**

* People hold ~7±2 items in working memory
  👉 Chunk information

### 7. **Cognitive Load Theory**

* Too much info reduces usability
  👉 Simplify, prioritize, remove noise

### 8. **Recognition over Recall**

* Easier to recognize than remember
  👉 Use dropdowns, autofill, suggestions

### 9. **Serial Position Effect**

* First & last items remembered best
  👉 Put key actions at top/bottom

---

## ⚡ Decision-Making & Behavior

### 10. **Jakob’s Law**

* Users expect familiar patterns
  👉 Follow conventions

### 11. **Law of Prägnanz (Gestalt)**

* People perceive simple shapes first
  👉 Clean layouts win

### 12. **Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule)**

* 80% of results from 20% features
  👉 Focus on core functionality

### 13. **Satisficing**

* Users pick “good enough” options
  👉 Make best option obvious

### 14. **Choice Overload**

* Too many options → decision paralysis
  👉 Limit choices

---

## 👥 Social & Emotional UX

### 15. **Social Proof**

* People follow others’ behavior
  👉 Reviews, ratings, testimonials

### 16. **Aesthetic-Usability Effect**

* Beautiful UI perceived as easier to use
  👉 Invest in visual design

### 17. **Peak-End Rule**

* Experience judged by peak & end
  👉 Optimize key moments + ending

### 18. **Loss Aversion**

* Fear of loss > desire for gain
  👉 “Don’t lose your progress”

---

## 🔁 Interaction Patterns

### 19. **Feedback Principle**

* Users need immediate response
  👉 Loading, success states, errors

### 20. **Direct Manipulation**

* Interactions should feel physical
  👉 Drag & drop, sliders

### 21. **Progressive Disclosure**

* Show info step-by-step
  👉 Reduce overwhelm

### 22. **Forgiveness & Undo**

* Allow mistakes
  👉 Undo, confirmations

---

## 📐 Layout & Visual Hierarchy

### 23. **Gestalt Principles**

* Proximity, similarity, continuity, closure
  👉 Group related items

### 24. **Whitespace (Negative Space)**

* Improves readability & focus
  👉 Don’t overcrowd UI

### 25. **Visual Hierarchy**

* Guide attention with size, color, contrast
  👉 Clear CTA priority

---

## 🧭 Navigation & Structure

### 26. **Information Scent**

* Users follow cues to find info
  👉 Clear labels, meaningful links

### 27. **3-Click Rule (Myth-ish but useful)**

* Users want quick access
  👉 Reduce friction

### 28. **Navigation Depth vs Breadth**

* Too deep = confusing
  👉 Prefer shallow structure

---

## 🧩 Forms & Input

### 29. **Error Prevention > Error Handling**

* Prevent mistakes before they happen
  👉 Validation, constraints

### 30. **Inline Validation**

* Immediate feedback on input
  👉 Reduce frustration

### 31. **Default Bias**

* Users stick with defaults
  👉 Choose smart defaults

---

## ⏱ Performance & Perception

### 32. **Perceived Performance**

* Feeling fast matters more than actual speed
  👉 Skeleton screens, progress bars

### 33. **Response Time Limits (Nielsen)**

* 0.1s = instant
* 1s = flow intact
* 10s = attention lost

---

## 🧠 Advanced / Less Common Phenomena

### 34. **Zeigarnik Effect**

* People remember unfinished tasks
  👉 Progress bars increase completion

### 35. **Goal-Gradient Effect**

* Motivation increases near completion
  👉 Show progress (e.g. 80%)

### 36. **IKEA Effect**

* Users value things they helped create
  👉 Customization features

### 37. **Endowment Effect**

* People value what they own
  👉 “Your dashboard”, personalization

### 38. **Cognitive Dissonance**

* Inconsistency causes discomfort
  👉 Keep UX consistent

---

## 🧪 Dark Patterns Awareness (Use Ethically)

### 39. **Scarcity Effect**

* Limited availability increases demand
  👉 Use honestly

### 40. **Urgency Bias**

* Time pressure drives action
  👉 Avoid fake timers

### 41. **Confirmshaming**

* Guilt-based opt-outs
  👉 Don’t manipulate users

---

# 🧩 Quick UX Heuristics Mapping

* **Reduce thinking** → Hick’s Law, Cognitive Load
* **Guide attention** → Visual Hierarchy, Von Restorff
* **Speed interaction** → Fitts’s Law
* **Increase trust** → Social Proof, Aesthetic Effect
* **Improve completion** → Zeigarnik, Goal Gradient
